# Why Offline-First ERP is Vital for Pakistani Factories

Operating a factory in industrial areas like Faisalabad, Sialkot, or Karachi is already full of challenges. From power outages to raw material delays, managers have a lot to deal with.

But there is one quiet productivity killer that many factories ignore: internet instability.

If your factory relies on cloud-based ERP software, a simple internet disconnection can bring your entire production floor to a complete halt.

## The Problem with Cloud-Only Software on the Factory Floor

Many modern software companies sell "cloud-only" solutions. They tell you that you can access your data from anywhere. While that sounds nice in a presentation, it is a disaster for daily factory work in Pakistan.

Here is why:

**1. Frequent Internet Outages**
Industrial zones often have poor fiber infrastructure. Heavy storms, local construction, or general network congestion can knock your internet out for hours or days. If your system is in the cloud, you cannot log attendance, scan inventory, or print dispatch slips.

**2. Speed Latency**
Every time a worker scans a barcode or inputs a production entry, a cloud system must send data to a remote server and wait for a response. In a busy packing or dispatch area, a 3-second delay per scan ruins worker efficiency.

**3. Data Cost**
Running large factory operations on backup 4G dongles during fiber cuts is expensive and unstable.
