# Preventing Material Theft in Your Factory using Smart CCTV

Security is a major concern for manufacturing facilities and warehouses. Whether you manage a textile unit, a packing facility, or a dry-fruit warehouse, your raw materials represent your biggest business investment.

The standard practice is to mount 16 or 32 security cameras across the ceiling, running long cables back to a central digital video recorder (DVR) box. 

However, here is a harsh industry truth: **standard security camera systems are completely passive.**

They do not protect your assets in real time. They merely act as a visual history book. When a high-value spare part, raw yarn bale, or chemical container goes missing, you are forced to spend hours scrubbing through recordings. By the time the theft is confirmed, the material has already left the gates, and your business has incurred the loss.

Instead of passive recording, using smart local AI camera zones provides an active, real-time barrier.

---

## The Failures of Traditional CCTV Systems

### 1. Security Guard Fatigue
Many factories employ guards to watch a wall of monitors. However, human attention span is short. Studies show that security operators miss up to 95% of screen activity after just 20 minutes of continuous monitoring. It is physically impossible for a guard to watch dozens of screens and detect a subtle motion at a distant back gate.

### 2. The Nightmare of False Alarms
Older recording boxes offer basic motion detection software. However, these systems trigger on anything: a passing cat, moving tree shadows, or blowing dust particles. Because these systems cause hundreds of false alarms per day, security personnel inevitably mute or ignore all alerts.

### 3. High Cloud Video Fees
Some modern systems offer smart alerts, but they require you to upload your live camera streams to the internet:
*   **High Internet Demand:** Streaming 16 high-definition security feeds consumes massive internet upload speed, which is expensive and unstable in industrial areas.
*   **Monthly Storage Costs:** Cloud camera providers charge steep monthly subscription fees per camera, ballooning operational expenses.
